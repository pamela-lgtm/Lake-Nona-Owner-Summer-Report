01 · The Origin Story

It started with a bet on an empty field

Lake Nona didn't grow by accident — it was designed, deliberately, as a city of the future. And the spark that lit it was a single, audacious act of generosity.

2005
Tavistock, the developer of Lake Nona, donated $12.5 million and 50 acres to UCF to launch a brand-new medical school — and the very beginnings of Medical City. It then challenged the community to match it.
2006
The community and the State of Florida answered. Between gifts, matching funds, and land value, UCF ultimately gathered well over $100 million, and the College of Medicine won approval.
2009
The first medical students arrived. Tavistock also donated the land for the neighboring research institute — seeding the entire cluster.
Today
That one donation grew into a 650-acre Medical City anchored by UCF's College of Medicine, Nemours Children's Hospital, the Orlando VA Medical Center, the UF College of Pharmacy, and a world-class research institute.

Your investment doesn't just sit near Lake Nona. It sits inside one of the most intentional communities ever built in Florida.

05 · Still Coming

The region isn't slowing down

The growth pipeline runs deep. A 377-unit Class-A residential community (Ellison Nona) is rising now, and in March 2026 Orlando approved a roughly 380-acre new development district (Dowden Central) that clears the way for still more housing, retail, and office on the southeast side of the master plan.
